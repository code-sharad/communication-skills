# Communication Skills Resources

A curated collection of resources to improve articulation, clarity, and structured communication.

---

## Core Frameworks

### 1. PREP Framework
**Best for**: Quick, structured responses

- **P**oint - State your main idea
- **R**eason - Explain why
- **E**xample - Provide evidence
- **P**oint - Restate conclusion

**Use when**: Answering questions, explaining ideas, making arguments

---

### 2. The Pyramid Principle
**Best for**: Complex explanations

Start with the conclusion, then provide supporting arguments grouped logically.

1. Main conclusion
2. Supporting argument 1 (with sub-points)
3. Supporting argument 2 (with sub-points)
4. Supporting argument 3 (with sub-points)

---

### 3. STAR Method
**Best for**: Storytelling and examples

- **S**ituation - Set the context
- **T**ask - Explain the challenge
- **A**ction - Describe what you did
- **R**esult - Share the outcome

## Word Choice Guidelines

### Replace Vague Words with Precise Ones

**Vague** | **Precise Alternatives**
:--- | :---
Good | Effective, beneficial, valuable, excellent
Bad | Ineffective, problematic, flawed, inadequate
Thing | Concept, element, factor, component, aspect
Stuff | Materials, items, resources, information
Nice | Pleasant, agreeable, thoughtful, considerate
Very | (Use stronger adjective instead)
Really | (Use stronger adjective instead)
A lot | Many, numerous, frequently, substantial

## Filler Words Reference

### Words to Eliminate

**Category** | **Examples** | **Replacement**
:--- | :--- | :---
Hesitation | um, uh, er | PAUSE (silence)
Unnecessaries | basically, actually, literally | Remove entirely
Uncertainties | kind of, sort of, like | Use precise words
Hedging | I think, I guess, maybe | State confidently or clarify
Repetition | you know, right, okay | Remove entirely

## Common Communication Patterns

### Pattern 1: Explaining a Concept
1. Define it simply
2. Explain why it matters
3. Give 1-2 examples
4. Address common confusion
5. Summarize

### Pattern 2: Giving an Opinion
1. State your position clearly
2. Provide 2-3 reasons
3. Acknowledge other perspectives
4. Reaffirm your position

### Pattern 3: Telling a Story
1. Set the scene (context)
2. Introduce the challenge
3. Describe your actions
4. Share the outcome
5. Extract the lesson

## Pronunciation & Clarity Tips

### Speak Clearly
- Open your mouth wider when speaking
- Enunciate consonants at word endings
- Don't rush - pace yourself
- Breathe between sentences

### Volume & Tone
- Speak loud enough to be heard easily
- Vary your tone to maintain interest
- Lower pitch conveys confidence
- Pause for emphasis

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: One-Sentence Clarity
Explain these in ONE clear sentence:
- What you do
- Your current project
- Your biggest strength
- Why communication matters

### Exercise 2: Two-Minute Drills
Speak for exactly 2 minutes on:
- A recent accomplishment
- A challenge you overcame
- Something you're learning
- A recommendation you'd give

### Exercise 3: Impromptu Speaking
Pick a random object. Speak about it for 1 minute using PREP.

### Exercise 4: Eliminate Fillers
Record yourself. Count filler words. Re-record, pausing instead.
